# Backgrounds

Every story has a beginning. Your character's background reveals where you came from, how you became an adventurer, and your place in the world. Your fighter might have been a courageous knight or a grizzled soldier. Your wizard could have been a sage or an artisan. Your rogue might have gotten by as a guild thief or commanded audiences as a jester.

Choosing a background provides you with important story cues about your character's identity. The most important question to ask about your background is *what changed*? Why did you stop doing whatever your background describes and start adventuring? Where did you get the money to purchase your starting gear, or, if you come from a wealthy background, why don't you have *more* money? How did you learn the skills of your class? What sets you apart from ordinary people who share your background?

The sample backgrounds in this chapter provide both concrete benefits (features, proficiencies, and languages) and roleplaying suggestions.

### Proficiencies

Each background gives a character proficiency in two skills (described in "Using Ability Scores").

In addition, most backgrounds give a character proficiency with one or more tools (detailed in "Equipment").

If a character would gain the same proficiency from two different sources, he or she can choose a different proficiency of the same kind (skill or tool) instead.

### Languages

Some backgrounds also allow characters to learn additional languages beyond those given by race. See "Languages."

### Equipment

Each background provides a package of starting equipment. If you use the optional rule to spend coin on gear, you do not receive the starting equipment from your background.

### Suggested Characteristics

A background contains suggested personal characteristics based on your background. You can pick characteristics, roll dice to determine them randomly, or use the suggestions as inspiration for characteristics of your own creation.

### Customizing a Background

You might want to tweak some of the features of a background so it better fits your character or the campaign setting. To customize a background, you can replace one feature with any other one, choose any two skills, and choose a total of two tool proficiencies or languages from the sample backgrounds. You can either use the equipment package from your background or spend coin on gear as described in the equipment section. (If you spend coin, you can't also take the equipment package suggested for your class.) Finally, choose two personality traits, one ideal, one bond, and one flaw. If you can't find a feature that matches your desired background, work with your GM to create one.

## Acolyte

You have spent your life in the service of a temple to a specific god or pantheon of gods. You act as an intermediary between the realm of the holy and the mortal world, performing sacred rites and offering sacrifices in order to conduct worshipers into the presence of the divine. You are not necessarily a cleric - performing sacred rites is not the same thing as channeling divine power.

Choose a god, a pantheon of gods, or some other quasi-divine being from among those listed in "Fantasy-Historical Pantheons" or those specified by your GM, and work with your GM to detail the nature of your religious service. Were you a lesser functionary in a temple, raised from childhood to assist the priests in the sacred rites? Or were you a high priest who suddenly experienced a call to serve your god in a different way? Perhaps you were the leader of a small cult outside of any established temple structure, or even an occult group that served a fiendish master that you now deny.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Insight, Religion

**Languages:** Two of your choice

**Equipment:** A holy symbol (a gift to you when you entered the priesthood), a prayer book or prayer wheel, 5 sticks of incense, vestments, a set of common clothes, and a pouch containing 15 gp

### Feature: Shelter of the Faithful

As an acolyte, you command the respect of those who share your faith, and you can perform the religious ceremonies of your deity. You and your adventuring companions can expect to receive free healing and care at a temple, shrine, or other established presence of your faith, though you must provide any material components needed for spells. Those who share your religion will support you (but only you) at a modest lifestyle.

You might also have ties to a specific temple dedicated to your chosen deity or pantheon, and you have a residence there. This could be the temple where you used to serve, if you remain on good terms with it, or a temple where you have found a new home. While near your temple, you can call upon the priests for assistance, provided the assistance you ask for is not hazardous and you remain in good standing with your temple.

### Suggested Characteristics

Acolytes are shaped by their experience in temples or other religious communities. Their study of the history and tenets of their faith and their relationships to temples, shrines, or hierarchies affect their mannerisms and ideals. Their flaws might be some hidden hypocrisy or heretical idea, or an ideal or bond taken to an extreme.

## Charlatan

You have always had a way with people. You know what makes them tick, you can tease out their hearts' desires after a few minutes of conversation, and with a few leading questions you can read them like they were children. You developed these skills early in life, when you learned that a fast tongue and a bright smile could wring coins out of even the tightest purse.

Whether you're an con artist who secretly conned an entire town, a swindler who made a living selling fake relics, or just a charming grifter with a taste for the high life, you've found a place for yourself in the world. The trick is keeping one step ahead of those you've wronged.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Deception, Sleight of Hand

**Tool Proficiencies:** Disguise kit, Forgery kit

**Equipment:** A set of fine clothes, a disguise kit, tools of the con of your choice (ten stoppered bottles filled with colored liquid, a set of weighted dice, a deck of marked cards, or a signet ring of an imaginary duke), and a belt pouch containing 15 gp

### Feature: False Identity

You have created a second identity that includes documentation, established acquaintances, and disguises that allow you to assume that persona. Additionally, you can forge documents including official papers and personal letters, as long as you have seen an example of the kind of document or the handwriting you are trying to copy.

## Criminal

You have a history of breaking the law and an underworld contact who helps you move in circles most people cannot reach. You might have been part of a guild of thieves, a fence who made a living selling stolen goods, or just a cutpurse with a talent for sneaking about and getting away with things.

Whatever your past, you've learned a few things that most people never do — how to spot a vulnerable mark, how to pick a pocket without being seen, how to case a location for valuables, and how to slip away when the heat is on. Those skills serve you well now that you've decided to put your talents to better use.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Deception, Stealth

**Tool Proficiencies:** One type of gaming set, Thieves' tools

**Equipment:** A crowbar, a set of dark common clothes including a hood, and a belt pouch containing 15 gp

### Feature: Criminal Contact

You have a reliable and trustworthy contact who acts as your liaison to a network of other criminals. You know how to get messages to and from your contact, even over great distances; specifically, you always know the local messenger service, and can send and receive messages through it without being traced.

## Entertainer

You thrive in front of an audience. You know how to entrance them, entertain them, and even inspire them. Your poetics can stir the hearts of those who hear you, awakening grief or joy, laughter or anger. Your music raises spirits or captures them in sorrow. Your dances whisk your observers away to other worlds, and your comedy punctures their pretensions.

Perhaps you were a member of a traveling troupe, a street performer who drew crowds in market squares, or an actor known for your legendary roles. You might still perform, or you might have turned your talents to a life of adventure, where your ability to entrance an audience serves you well in any negotiation or pitched battle.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Acrobatics, Performance

**Tool Proficiencies:** Disguise kit, One type of musical instrument

**Equipment:** A musical instrument (one of your choice), the favor of an admirer (love letter, lock of hair, or trinket), a costume, and a belt pouch containing 15 gp

### Feature: By Popular Demand

You can always find a place to perform, usually in an inn or tavern but possibly with a circus, at a theater, or even in a noble's court. At such a place, you receive free lodging and food of a modest or comfortable standard (depending on the quality of the establishment), as long as you perform each night.

## Folk Hero

You come from a humble background but you are destined for so much more. Already the people of your home village regard you as their champion, and your destiny calls you to stand against the tyrants and monsters that threaten the common folk everywhere. Your past is littered with heroic acts that have won you the love and admiration of ordinary people, though some of your deeds might be exaggerated in the retelling.

You might have been a farmer whose flock was attacked by bandits, a young cobber who witnessed a murder and reported it to the authorities, or an orphan raised by a wise old wizard who taught you the basics of magic. Whatever your story, you've come to believe that the common folk deserve a champion.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Animal Handling, Survival

**Tool Proficiencies:** One type of artisan's tools, Vehicles (land)

**Equipment:** A set of artisan's tools (one of your choice), a shovel, an iron pot, a set of common clothes, and a belt pouch containing 10 gp

### Feature: Rustic Hospitality

You have a place to stay wherever you go among ordinary folk — farmers, shepherds, goatherds, hunters, and the like. Common folk will put you up for the night, feed you, and generally treat you as one of their own, provided you don't bring trouble to their door.

## Guild Artisan

You are a member of an artisan's guild, skilled in a particular field and closely associated with other artisans. You are well established in the world, with a secure place in a social class that values your labor. Your guild is your lifeline — the source of your training, your tools, your connections, and your livelihood.

You might have been a baker, a blacksmith, a carpenter, a jeweler, a mason, a potter, a weaver, or a woodworker. Whatever your trade, you've learned the value of hard work, the wisdom of saving for the future, and the importance of maintaining good relationships with those who share your craft.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Insight, Persuasion

**Tool Proficiencies:** One type of artisan's tools

**Languages:** One of your choice

**Equipment:** A set of artisan's tools (one of your choice), a letter of introduction from your guild, a set of traveler's clothes, and a belt pouch containing 15 gp

### Feature: Guild Membership

As an established and respected member of a guild, you can rely on certain benefits that membership provides. You have access to guild lodging in towns and cities where your guild has a presence, and can receive free healing and care at a guildhouse. Your guild also helps you find work, and may even be able to pull strings to get you an audience with a local noble or guildmaster.

## Hermit

You lived apart from the world, removed from its noise and bustle, in a place where you could contemplate the deeper questions of existence. Whether you lived in a small cottage at the edge of a wood, in a cave overlooking the sea, or in a forgotten temple in the middle of the jungle, your seclusion gave you a unique perspective on the world.

You might have been a hermit who sought spiritual enlightenment, a scholar pursuing arcane knowledge in isolation, or a former adventurer looking for peace after a life of danger. Whatever your reason for withdrawing from society, you've emerged from your isolation with a clarity of mind and a sense of purpose that others lack.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Medicine, Religion

**Tool Proficiencies:** Herbalism kit

**Languages:** One of your choice

**Equipment:** A scroll case stuffed with notes from your studies or prayers, a winter blanket, a set of common clothes, a herbalism kit, and a belt pouch containing 5 gp

### Feature: Discovery

Your solitary life gave you ample opportunity to study something unusual — a peculiar ritual, a forgotten piece of lore, or some other esoteric knowledge. You know the secret of how to perform a particular ritual or how to find a particular obscure place, and you can use this knowledge to help yourself or others.

## Noble

You were born into privilege. The finest foods, the finest clothes, the finest education, and the finest companions — you took them all for granted as a child, but as you've grown you've come to appreciate the responsibilities that come with wealth and power. You know what it takes to maintain a household and a reputation, and you've learned the social graces that smooth the path to success.

You might be the scion of a noble house, the heir to a wealthy merchant family, or a junior member of a royal court. Whatever your station, you've learned the value of appearances, the importance of making connections, and the art of leading others.

**Skill Proficiencies:** History, Persuasion

**Tool Proficiencies:** One type of gaming set

**Languages:** One of your choice

**Equipment:** A set of fine clothes, a signet ring, a scroll of pedigree, and a purse containing 25 gp

### Feature: Position of Privilege

Thanks to your noble birth, people are inclined to think the best of you. You are welcome in high society, and people assume you have the right to be wherever you are. Common folk make way for you, and servants are polite and accommodating.

## Outlander

You grew up in the wild, far from the comforts of town and technology. You've watched the moon rise over vast plains, seen the aurora dance across the sky, and felt the biting cold of winter against your skin. The wilds are your home, and you carry its rhythms in your blood.

You might have been a nomad who roamed the steppes, a hunter who tracked game through ancient forests, or a tribesperson who lived by the bounty of the sea. Whatever your origin, you learned to survive in the wilderness, to read the signs of nature, and to find your way by the stars.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Athletics, Survival

**Tool Proficiencies:** One type of musical instrument

**Languages:** One of your choice

**Equipment:** A staff, a hunting trap, a trophy from an animal you killed, a set of traveler's clothes, and a belt pouch containing 10 gp

### Feature: Wanderer

You have an excellent memory for geography and can always recall the general layout of terrain, settlements, and other features around you. In addition, you can find food and water for yourself and up to five other people each day, provided the land offers berries, small game, water, and the like.

## Sage

You spent years learning the lore of the multiverse. You scoured manuscripts, studied scrolls, and listened to the greatest experts on the subjects that interested you. Your efforts have made you a master in your fields of study, and you've learned enough to separate truth from falsehood in matters of magic, history, religion, and the like.

You might have been a student at a great library, an apprentice to a wandering sage, or even a self-taught scholar who taught yourself from ancient ruins. Whatever your path, you've accumulated a wealth of knowledge that sets you apart from the common folk.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Arcana, History

**Languages:** Two of your choice

**Equipment:** A bottle of black ink, a quill, a small knife, a letter from a dead colleague posing a question you have not yet been able to answer, a set of common clothes, and a belt pouch containing 10 gp

### Feature: Researcher

When you attempt to learn or recall a piece of lore, if you don't know the information, you often know where to obtain it. Usually, you can find a library, a scholar, or a wizard who knows the information you seek, though the information might be locked behind price, persuasion, or even a quest.

## Sailor

You have been on the waves since you were old enough to walk the deck of a ship. The sea is your home, and you know the rhythms of the wind, the tides, and the creatures that swim beneath the surface. Whether you were a fisherman, a pirate, a merchant sailor, or a navy veteran, you've learned to handle a ship and to fight when boarding actions come.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Athletics, Perception

**Tool Proficiencies:** Navigator's tools, Vehicles (water)

**Equipment:** A belaying pin (club), 50 feet of silk rope, a lucky charm such as a rabbit foot or a small stone with a hole in the center, a set of common clothes, and a belt pouch containing 10 gp

### Feature: Ship's Passage

When you need to, you can secure free passage on a sailing ship for yourself and your adventuring companions. You are familiar with the operation of a ship and can serve as a member of the crew in any role you choose.

## Soldier

War has been your life for as long as you care to remember. You trained as a youth, studied the use of weapons and armor, learned basic survival skills, including how to stay alive on the battlefield. You might have been part of a standing army, a mercenary company, or a militia defending your hometown from monsters. The life of a soldier is hard, but you have learned to cope with its demands.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Athletics, Intimidation

**Tool Proficiencies:** One type of gaming set, Vehicles (land)

**Equipment:** An insignia of rank, a trophy taken from a fallen enemy (a dagger, broken blade, or piece of a banner), a set of bone dice or a deck of cards, a set of common clothes, and a belt pouch containing 10 gp

### Feature: Military Rank

You have a military rank from your career as a soldier. Soldiers loyal to your former military organization still recognize your authority and rank, and they will defer to you if your rank is higher than theirs. You can invoke your rank to impose your will on soldiers in the organization, and they will follow your orders as long as your commands don't contradict their standing orders.

## Urchin

You grew up on the streets alone, orphaned, and poor. You had no one to watch over you, no one to shield you from the ever-present dangers of the world, and so you learned quickly to take care of yourself. You learned to live by your wits and to survive on whatever you could scrounge, beg, or steal.

You might have been part of a gang of street urchins, a lone pickpocket working the market crowds, or simply a homeless orphan who survived by begging. Whatever your story, you've grown up hard and fast, and you've learned that the world owes you nothing.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Sleight of Hand, Stealth

**Tool Proficiencies:** Disguise kit, Thieves' tools

**Equipment:** A small knife, a map of the city you grew up in, a pet mouse, a token to remember your parents by, a set of common clothes, and a belt pouch containing 10 gp

### Feature: City Secrets

You know the secret patterns and flow of cities and can find passages through the urban sprawl that others would miss. When you are not in combat, you can travel between any two locations in the city twice as fast as your speed would normally allow.
